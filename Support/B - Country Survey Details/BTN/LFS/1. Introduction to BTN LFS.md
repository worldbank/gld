## What is the BTN LFS/QLFS?

The Bhutan Labour Force Survey (LFS) is the main household survey used to collect information on labour market activities and employment conditions in Bhutan. The survey is conducted by the National Statistics Bureau (NSB) of Bhutan. Starting in 2023, the survey was revised into the Quarterly Labour Force Survey (QLFS), with data collected and released by quarter.

The Bhutan LFS/QLFS is used to produce official labour market indicators, including labour force participation, employment, unemployment, and characteristics of employed and non-employed individuals. The GLD harmonization currently covers the annual LFS rounds and the more recent quarterly QLFS rounds.

## What does the BTN LFS/QLFS cover?

The Bhutan LFS/QLFS collects information on household and individual demographic characteristics, education, labour force status, employment, unemployment, reasons for being outside the labour force, occupation, industry, working hours, and other employment-related characteristics.

For the GLD harmonization, the covered files include annual LFS rounds for earlier years and quarterly QLFS rounds from 2023 onward. The years and sample sizes harmonized for GLD are:

| 2018 | 8,855 | 37,113 |

## Where can the data be found?

The Bhutan LFS/QLFS microdata and documentation are provided by the National Statistics Bureau (NSB) of Bhutan. Some public-use files and documentation are available through the NSB website, while other files may be shared directly by NSB or through World Bank internal channels.

The harmonized GLD files should be accessed through the relevant World Bank / GLD data storage systems. Users should refer to the documentation stored in the country utilities or documentation folder for the specific survey year being used.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The Bhutan LFS/QLFS uses a stratified multi-stage sampling design. For the quarterly survey years, the sampling design was revised after the introduction of the QLFS. The National Statistics Bureau clarified that the `cstr_code` variable in the 2024–2025 QLFS is a collapsed stratum code used for variance estimation. It should not be treated as the PSU.

For survey design purposes, the Primary Sampling Unit (PSU) should be based on the Enumeration Area (EA) variable. In the 2024–2025 QLFS, the EA variable should be used as the PSU. For the 2021–2022 LFS, the EA code is available for urban areas. In rural areas, the EA code corresponds to the `clcode` / Chiwog or LAP code, and the rural PSU should therefore be based on `clcode`.

The collapsed stratum variable `cstr_code` in the QLFS identifies variance estimation strata rather than original sampling clusters. Its structure includes information on the replicate or quarter, urban/rural/thromde classification, dzongkhag, and assigned collapsed stratum.

## What is the geographic significance level?

The Bhutan LFS/QLFS is nationally representative and supports reporting by urban/rural areas and major administrative/geographic domains used in the official survey design. For recent QLFS rounds, the documentation indicates coverage across Bhutan’s dzongkhags and thromdes, with stratification by urban and rural areas.

Users should consult the official survey documentation for each year before producing estimates below the national level, especially for subnational or quarterly estimates. For the QLFS years, quarter-specific estimates should be interpreted in line with the official sampling design and weighting structure.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Transition from annual LFS to quarterly QLFS

The Bhutan LFS became a quarterly survey starting in 2023. This change affects the structure of the raw files, the identifier variables, and the treatment of the `wave` variable in the harmonized data. For the quarterly years, `wave` is used to identify the quarter.

### Household and personal identifiers

The identifier structure changes over time.

For 2018–2022, the raw data include both household and personal identifiers. The household identifier is based on `interview__key` / `interview__id`, and the personal or intra-household identifier is `hh_mem_rost__id`.

Starting in 2023, after the survey became quarterly, the raw data include a household identifier but no clear personal or intra-household identifier. For 2025, a household identifier is available, but no clear personal identifier is observed.

For 2024, the household identifier information is partially missing or inconsistent across quarters. Q1 includes both `interview__key` and `interview__id`; Q2 includes only `interview__key`; Q3 includes only `interview__id`; and the original Q4 2024 public-use file did not include a clear household/interview identifier. NSB confirmed that the unique identifiers were inadvertently omitted from the Q4 2024 public-use file and that an updated Q4 2024 dataset with household identifiers would be shared.

### Occupation classification: BSCO and ISCO

The Bhutan LFS/QLFS uses the Bhutan Standard Classification of Occupations (BSCO), which is a national adaptation of ISCO-08. BSCO is largely aligned with ISCO-08, but some Bhutan-specific or more detailed national codes do not correspond directly to valid ISCO-08 unit groups.

In the GLD harmonization, occupation codes are treated as ISCO-08 where directly aligned. BSCO-specific codes are mapped to the closest valid ISCO-08 category or to the next highest valid group where appropriate. For example, Bhutan-specific armed forces codes are mapped to the corresponding ISCO-08 armed forces categories, and national detailed teaching, services, and professional categories are mapped to the closest valid ISCO-08 group. Unknown or unclassifiable codes are set to missing where no supporting label or documentation is available.

A separate note or code comments document the specific BSCO-to-ISCO treatments used in the harmonization.

### Industry classification: BSIC and ISIC

The Bhutan LFS/QLFS uses the Bhutan Standard Industrial Classification (BSIC), which is aligned with ISIC Rev. 4. In the GLD harmonization, BSIC industry codes are therefore treated as ISIC Rev. 4 where directly aligned.

Some older files store ISIC/BSIC codes numerically, which removes leading zeros from valid ISIC Rev. 4 codes. For example, `0111` may appear as `111`, and `0810` may appear as `810`. These codes are converted to four-digit strings during harmonization to preserve the correct ISIC Rev. 4 structure.

A small number of invalid or ambiguous industry codes are reviewed using raw labels and, where possible, mapped to the closest valid ISIC Rev. 4 category. Where no supporting information is available, the code is set to missing.

### Survey design variables

For 2024–2025, `cstr_code` should be treated as a collapsed stratum code for variance estimation, not as the PSU. The EA variable should be used as the PSU. For 2021–2022, the EA variable should be used as the PSU for urban areas, while `clcode` / Chiwog or LAP code should be used as the PSU for rural areas.
