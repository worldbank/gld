## What is the JOR LFS?

The Jordan Labor Force Survey (LFS) is a labor and unemployment survey conducted by the General Statistics Department (Department of Statistics) of Jordan. The survey is carried out across four rounds per year — in February, May, August, and November — and the data from all rounds are merged to produce annual indicators at the national level. The published data cover the Jordanian population only and exclude households residing in collective dwellings.

## What does the JOR LFS cover?

The Jordan LFS is a labor force survey collecting information on employment and unemployment. The survey covers approximately 53,000 households distributed across all governorates of the Kingdom. The sample includes households residing in conventional dwellings only, and the published data are for the Jordanian population only. ## Where can the data be found?

The microdata for the Jordan LFS are not publicly available for download. Access to the data is restricted and can only be obtained through a formal data request to the Department of Statistics of Jordan. World Bank staff seeking access should get in touch with the Jobs Group for further guidance on the data request process.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The JO LFS uses a stratified multi-stage cluster sampling design, ensuring representation at the level of the Kingdom, its governorates, its three regions, and both urban and rural areas. The sample is based on the framework provided by the General Census of Population and Housing for the year 2004.

The Kingdom was divided into strata, where each city with a population of 100,000 or more was classified as a large city — six such cities were identified. Each governorate, with the exception of the six large cities, was divided into rural and urban areas, with the remaining urban area in each governorate treated as an independent stratum. Given the significant variation in socio-economic characteristics in large cities and urban areas, each stratum was further divided into four sub-strata based on socio-economic characteristics derived from the 2004 Census, with the aim of producing homogeneous strata.

The sample was selected in two stages:

- **Stage One:** Primary sampling units (PSUs) were drawn using systematic probability proportional to size (PPS) sampling, where the number of households in each cluster served as the size measure. Within each stratum, PSUs were arranged first by geographical characteristics and then by socio-economic characteristics to ensure a good spread of the sample.

- **Stage Two:** Selected clusters were updated, and a fixed number of 10 households per cluster were then selected using systematic random sampling as the final sampling units.

The master sample consists of 40 replicates, each comprising 167 primary sampling units. For each survey round, eight replicates from the master sample were used, resulting in 1,336 PSUs per round and a total of 5,344 clusters across all four rounds.

## What is the geographic significance level?

The survey is designed to produce statistically significant results at the level of the country, its three regions (governorates), and urban/rural breakdowns. That is, results can be reported by gender, urban/rural sector, region, and governorate.

## Other noteworthy aspects

- The survey covers only the Jordanian population; non-Jordanian residents are excluded from the published data.
- Households in collective residences (e.g., military barracks, dormitories) are excluded from the sample.
- The annual indicators are derived by merging data from four quarterly rounds conducted in February, May, August, and November.
