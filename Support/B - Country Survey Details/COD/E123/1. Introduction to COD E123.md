# Introduction to Democratic Republic of the Congo Enquete 1-2-3 (COD E123)

## What is the COD E123?

The Enquete 1-2-3 (E123) is a multi-phase household survey program implemented in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The harmonized rounds currently covered in the GLD correspond to Phase 1, the employment phase of the 1-2-3 system. In practical terms, the COD E123 rounds used in the GLD are employment-focused household surveys rather than stand-alone labor force surveys.

## What does the COD E123 cover?

The COD E123 harmonized rounds contain information on demographic characteristics, education, and labor market activities over the past 7 days. The harmonized years and sample sizes currently used in the GLD are as follows:

| Year | HH count | Individual count |
|---|---|---|
| 2004 | 13,215 | 72,685 |
| 2012 | 21,454 | 111,679 |

## Where can the data be found?

The datasets are not accessible to the public and researchers have to request the data from the Institut National de la Statistique (INS) of the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The World Bank has been granted access to the datasets. If you work or are part of the World Bank Group, kindly contact the Jobs Group with a formal request for access to gld@worldbank.org.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The survey uses a stratified multi-stage sampling design. In the earlier 2004-2005 implementation, the survey was rolled out first in Kinshasa and then in the rest of the country. By 2012, the survey followed a more integrated national design. For further details, users should consult the survey documentation for the relevant years.

## What is the geographic significance level?

The level of representativeness changes across survey rounds. In the earlier 2004-2005 materials, the main reporting disaggregation is Kinshasa and urban-rural residence. From 2012 onwards, the survey supports national and province-level estimates, with results also commonly disaggregated by urban and rural residence. This reflects the change from the earlier staged rollout to the more integrated 2012 design.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Employment: Industry classification

The harmonized COD E123 rounds use different source classifications across years. The survey materials reviewed for these rounds do not explicitly identify a named industry classification system. In the GLD harmonization, the survey coding is mapped to ISIC Rev. 3.1 for 2004 and ISIC Rev. 4 for 2012.

| Year(s) | Classification in survey | ISIC revision mapped to | Level of compatibility |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| 2004 | Unknown | ISIC Rev. 3.1 | 2 digits |
| 2012 | Unknown | ISIC Rev. 4 | 2 digits |

### Employment: Occupation classification

The harmonized COD E123 rounds use different source classifications across years. The survey materials reviewed for these rounds do not explicitly identify a named occupation classification system. In the GLD harmonization, the survey coding is mapped to ISCO-88 for both rounds.

| Year(s) | Classification in survey | ISCO revision mapped to | Level of compatibility |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| 2004 | Unknown | ISCO-88 | 2 digits |
| 2012 | Unknown | ISCO-88 | 3 digits |

### Wages

The COD E123 provides monthly income information not only for wage workers, but also for workers paid through commissions, business profits, and in-kind remuneration. Moreover, the survey allows income to be reported either as an exact value or as a range. In the GLD harmonization, exact reported amounts are used directly. When income is reported only in ranges, the standard GLD approach is to impute a point value using the mean exact earnings of comparable workers in the same bracket, clustered by sex, urban-rural residence, occupation, and broad industry group, with fallback to simpler cells when donor groups are sparse.

### Geography and timing

The delivered 2004 and 2012 microdata use the older 11-province structure. The 2012 harmonized file also shows that interviews are concentrated in late 2012 with a smaller spillover into early 2013.

### Questionnaire and raw-data differences

There are cases in which the raw data are less detailed than the questionnaire would suggest. The GLD harmonization only codes variables that are clearly supported by the delivered data. For this reason, some variables that appear possible from the questionnaire remain missing in the harmonized output when the corresponding raw detail is not sufficiently reliable or complete.
