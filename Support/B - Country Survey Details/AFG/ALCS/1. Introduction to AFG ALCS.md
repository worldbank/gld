## What is the AFG ALCS?

The Afghanistan Living Conditions Survey (ALCS) is a national household survey implemented by the Central Statistics Organization (CSO) of Afghanistan. It is not a stand-alone labour force survey in the narrow sense. Rather, it is a broader living conditions survey that includes a labour module alongside household roster, education, migration, disability, and other socioeconomic content.

## What does the AFG ALCS cover?

The AFG ALCS covers demographic characteristics, education, migration, disability, and labour market activities. The harmonized years and sample sizes used in the GLD harmonization are as follows:

| Year | HH count | Individual count |
|---|---|---|
| 2013 | 20,786 | 157,262 |
| 2016 | 19,838 | 155,680 |

The harmonized survey years correspond to the first year of fieldwork for each round.

## Where can the data be found?

The datasets are not accessible to the public and researchers have to request the data from the Central Statistics Organization (CSO) / National Statistics and Information Authority (NSIA) of Afghanistan. The World Bank has been granted access to the datasets, if you work or are part of the World Bank Group, kindly contact the Jobs Group with a formal request for access to gld@worldbank.org.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The ALCS rounds harmonized in the GLD use a stratified cluster design intended to produce estimates that are representative at the national and provincial levels, while also covering the nomadic Kuchi population and Shamsi-calendar seasonality. In both 2013-14 and 2016-17, the sample is organised into 35 strata: 34 provincial strata and one Kuchi stratum.

For the resident population, the survey uses a two-stage cluster design within provinces. Enumeration Areas (EAs) are selected as Primary Sampling Units (PSUs) with probability proportional to size, and ten households are then selected in each cluster as Ultimate Sampling Units (USUs). In the 2013-14 round, some rural EAs that covered more than one village effectively introduced an additional village-selection step before household selection. The Kuchi sample is selected separately using the Kuchi frame and a clustered design with the same household cluster size.

## What is the significance level?

The survey design supports national and provincial estimates, and the reports also treat season and Kuchi status as important dimensions of representativeness. However, the labour-market chapter in the official ALCS reports mainly presents labour indicators by residence category, sex, age group, and season, rather than as a full set of province-level labour tables. In practice, the most detailed geographic breakdown consistently reported for labour indicators is residence: urban, rural, and Kuchi.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Labour status and job attachment

The ALCS labour module uses a broad work concept in the 7-day reference period. Reported work in wage employment, household business activities, and farm or livestock work is treated as employment in the GLD harmonization.

In the 2013-14 round, temporary absence is effectively the survey's job-attachment test. Respondents who report being only temporarily absent from work are routed directly to the main-job section of the questionnaire, while those who are not temporarily absent are routed to the later search-and-availability branch. The delivered questionnaire does not show a separate follow-up on expected return date, duration of absence, or continued pay before that routing decision. For this reason, the GLD harmonization treats temporary absence as sufficient job attachment in 2013-14.

In the 2016-17 round, unemployment is coded conservatively as not employed, available for work, and either looking for work or already having found work to start later.

### Education: Islamic school mapping

In the 2013-14 ALCS, `q_10_5` identifies the schooling track and `q_10_6` records the highest completed grade within that track. In the GLD harmonization, these two variables are used jointly so that the resulting education ladder is mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive, including cases with no completed grade (`q_10_6 = 0`).

| `q_10_5` code | Schooling track | Observed `q_10_6` range | GLD `educat7` treatment |
|---|---|---|
| `1` | Primary | `0-6` | `0 -> 1`, `1-5 -> 2`, `6 -> 3` |
| `2` | Lower secondary | `6-9` | `6 -> 3`, `7-9 -> 4` |
| `3` | Upper secondary | `9-12` | `9 -> 4`, `10-12 -> 5` |
| `4` | Teacher college | `12-14` | `12 -> 5`, `13-14 -> 6` |
| `5` | University / technical college | `12-16` | `12 -> 5`, `13-14 -> 6`, `15-16 -> 7` |
| `6` | Post-graduate | `16-19` | `7` |
| `7` | Islamic school | `0-14` | `0 -> 1`, `1-5 -> 2`, `6 -> 3`, `7-9 -> 4`, `10-12 -> 5`, `13-14 -> 6` |

Islamic school is coded as `q_10_5 = 7`. These cases are not left as a separate survey-specific education category in the GLD output. Instead, the harmonization uses the Islamic-school grade reported in `q_10_6` to place them on the standard GLD education ladder. In practice, grade `0` is treated as no completed grade, grades `1-5` as primary incomplete, grade `6` as primary complete, grades `7-9` as lower secondary, grades `10-12` as upper secondary, and grades `13-14` as post-secondary non-university. This affects 265 observations and avoids leaving Islamic schooling as an uncoded residual category.

Teacher college and university / technical college are also interpreted jointly with `q_10_6`, so that respondents with grade `12` remain at upper-secondary completion, while only those with post-secondary completed grades are moved above upper secondary in the GLD ladder.

### Employment: Industry Classification

In both harmonized ALCS rounds, the industry codes behave like grouped survey classifications aligned with ISIC Revision 2 at the first two digits. However, the raw industry codes do not support a complete exact 4-digit ISIC correspondence across the full code frame. For this reason, the GLD harmonization sets the industry classification to ISIC Revision 2 and codes `industrycat_isic` conservatively from the first two digits.

### Employment: Occupation Classification

The occupation codes in the harmonized ALCS rounds generally align with ISCO-08. The 2013-14 round includes a small number of inconsistent detailed occupation codes, which are recoded to missing in the harmonization, whereas the 2016-17 round aligns cleanly with ISCO-08.
