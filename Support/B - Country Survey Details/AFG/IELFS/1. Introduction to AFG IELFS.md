## What is the AFG IELFS?

The Afghanistan Income, Expenditure and Labour Force Survey (IELFS) 2019-20 is a nationally representative household survey implemented by the National Statistics and Information Authority (NSIA) of Afghanistan. It was Afghanistan's flagship nationally representative household survey. It is not a stand-alone labour force survey in the narrow sense. Rather, it is a broader household survey that includes a detailed labour module alongside household roster, education, disability, and expenditure-related content.

## What does the AFG IELFS cover?

The AFG IELFS covers demographic characteristics, education, disability, and labour market activities. In the labour module, the questionnaire captures current work during the last 7 days, temporary absence from work, availability and search, and main-job and second-job information.

The harmonized survey used in the GLD workflow has the following sample size:

| Year | HH count | Individual count |
|---|---|---|
| 2019 | 18,344 | 136,848 |

The first wave of the survey was conducted in person from October 2019 to September 2020. In the GLD harmonization, the survey is referred to as 2019 following the standard practice of naming by the first survey year.

## Where can the data be found?

The datasets are not accessible to the public and researchers have to request the data from the National Statistics and Information Authority (NSIA). The World Bank has been granted access to the datasets, if you work or are part of the World Bank Group, kindly contact the Jobs Group with a formal request for access to gld@worldbank.org.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The official IE&LFS 2020 report states that the survey design was intended to produce estimates representative at the national and provincial levels, for the Kuchi population, and across Shamsi calendar seasons. The design used 35 strata: 34 province strata and one Kuchi stratum.

For the non-Kuchi population, households were selected through a two-stage cluster design within province. In the first stage, Enumeration Areas (EAs) were selected as Primary Sampling Units (PSUs) with probability proportional to size. To account for potential inaccessibility, the report explains that an extended sample of about 1.1 times the required number of EAs was first drawn, with implicit stratification by urban-rural residence and district, and that the target sample was then selected from that extended sample. In the second stage, 15 households were selected by simple random sampling in each cluster: 10 target households and 5 reserve households.

The report indicates a planned annual sample of 2,040 clusters and 20,400 households, corresponding to 170 clusters and 1,700 households per month. For the Kuchi stratum, 60 clusters and 600 households were allocated across the winter and summer seasons. Sample weights were calculated using official NSIA population totals for 1398 (2019-20).

Fieldwork ran from early October 2019 to late September 2020. The report also notes that some sampled clusters could not be visited because of insecurity and other field constraints, so reserve clusters were used in their place when possible.

## What is the significance level?

The official IE&LFS 2020 report presents the survey as nationally and provincially representative, with separate coverage of the Kuchi population and seasonal coverage through fieldwork spread over the year. The report also presents many indicators by urban, rural, and Kuchi residence.

At the same time, the report documents field constraints. It notes that reserve clusters were used where some target clusters could not be visited, and that 329 out of 399 districts were covered in practice. For this reason, the GLD treats province as the most defensible subnational reporting level in the CSD. The raw data include district identifiers, but the available documentation does not justify treating the survey as district-representative.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Labour status and job attachment

The 7-day labour status variable is built from direct work screens, a direct temporary-absence question, and the search-and-availability branch. In the questionnaire available to the GLD, temporary absence is treated as the operative job-attachment test. The instrument does not show a separate follow-up on expected return date, duration of absence, or continued pay before routing respondents onward. For this reason, respondents reporting temporary absence are treated as employed in the GLD harmonization.

Unemployment is coded conservatively as not employed, available for work, and either looking for work or already having found a future job start. Potential labour force is limited to those outside the labour force who are either available but not looking, or looking but not available.

### Industry classification

The questionnaire includes an industry code field together with a description field for the respondent's activity, but the raw industry variable in the delivered data does not have value labels and the documentation package available to the GLD does not identify the classification by name.

Based on the structure of the raw codes, the closest international correspondence is ISIC Revision 4. However, the raw industry coding behaves like a grouped survey classification rather than a defensible exact 4-digit ISIC classification throughout. For this reason, the GLD harmonization sets `isic_version = "isic_4"` but codes `industrycat_isic` conservatively using the first two digits plus `00`.

### Occupation classification

The questionnaire likewise includes an occupation code field and a description field, but the raw occupation variable also lacks value labels in the delivered data. Comparing the observed code structure to the GLD helper files shows that ISCO-2008 fits the data much better than ISCO-1988.

Even so, the raw occupation scheme is not fully defensible at detailed 4 digits across the survey as a whole. To avoid mixing exact-looking detailed occupation codes with grouped fallback codes, the GLD harmonization applies a uniform grouped approach and codes `occup_isco` using the first two digits plus `00` whenever possible.
