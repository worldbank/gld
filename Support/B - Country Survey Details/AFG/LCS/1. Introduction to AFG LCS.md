## What is the AFG LCS?

The Afghanistan Living Conditions Survey (LCS) is a nationally representative household survey implemented by the Central Statistics Organization (CSO) of Afghanistan, later the National Statistics and Information Authority (NSIA). It is not a stand-alone labour force survey. Rather, it is a broader living conditions survey that combines household, demographic, education, migration, disability, and labour-market modules.

The GLD harmonization currently covers the 2013-14 and 2016-17 rounds of the survey series.

## What does the AFG LCS cover?

The AFG LCS covers demographic characteristics, education, migration, disability, and labour market activities. In the labour module, the survey collects information on work during the last 7 days, temporary absence from work, availability and search, employment characteristics, and selected annual labour indicators.

The harmonized years and sample sizes used in the GLD harmonization are as follows:

| Year | HH count | Individual count |
|---|---|---|
| 2013 | 20,786 | 157,262 |
| 2016 | 19,838 | 155,680 |

## Where can the data be found?

The datasets are not accessible to the public and researchers have to request the data from the Afghanistan National Statistics and Information Authority (NSIA). The World Bank has been granted access to the datasets, if you work or are part of the World Bank Group, kindly contact the Jobs Group with a formal request for access to gld@worldbank.org.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The AFG LCS rounds covered in the GLD use a stratified cluster sampling design with 35 strata: the 34 provinces of Afghanistan and one Kuchi stratum. For the settled population, enumeration areas were selected as primary sampling units with probability proportional to size, and 10 households were selected within each sampled cluster.

The 2013-14 and 2016-17 rounds follow the same overall design. One implementation difference is that in 2013-14, some sampled rural enumeration areas that contained more than one village effectively introduced an additional village-selection step before household selection.

## What is the significance level?

The survey design supports national and provincial estimates. In the official labour reporting, labour indicators are presented most consistently by residence category, especially urban, rural, and Kuchi, rather than by province.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Labour status and job attachment

The GLD codes labour status from the concept used in the survey questionnaire. In the 2013-14 round, the questionnaire uses a direct temporary-absence question as the operative job-attachment test. Respondents who report that they usually work but were only temporarily absent are routed directly into the main-job module, while those who do not report temporary absence are routed to the availability and search branch. For this reason, temporary absence is treated as attachment to a job in the GLD harmonization for 2013-14.

In the 2016-17 round, unemployment is likewise built from the survey's own search and availability questions. The GLD does not impose an external job-attachment rule beyond the routing observed in the questionnaire.

### Education: schooling track and Islamic school mapping

In the 2013-14 round, the education ladder requires joint use of two variables: `q_10_5`, which identifies the schooling track, and `q_10_6`, which records the highest completed grade within that track. The observed cross-tab pattern shows that grade ranges are track-specific rather than universal. For this reason, the GLD does not use `q_10_6` on its own, but interprets it together with `q_10_5`.

| `q_10_5` | Schooling track | Observed `q_10_6` range | GLD `educat7` treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Primary | 0-6 | `0 -> 1`, `1-5 -> 2`, `6 -> 3` |
| 2 | Lower secondary | 6-9 | `6 -> 3`, `7-9 -> 4` |
| 3 | Upper secondary | 9-12 | `9 -> 4`, `10-12 -> 5` |
| 4 | Teacher college | 12-14 | `12 -> 5`, `13-14 -> 6` |
| 5 | University / technical college | 12-16 | `12 -> 5`, `13-14 -> 6`, `15-16 -> 7` |
| 6 | Post-graduate | 16-19 | `16-19 -> 7` |
| 7 | Islamic school | 0-14 | `0 -> 1`, `1-5 -> 2`, `6 -> 3`, `7-9 -> 4`, `10-12 -> 5`, `13-14 -> 6` |

This matters most for Islamic school, which is identified by `q_10_5 == 7`. Rather than leaving these observations as a survey-specific residual category, the GLD maps them into the standard education ladder by completed grade. This affects 265 observations in the 2013-14 round.

### Industry classification

The industry variables in the AFG LCS align most closely with ISIC Revision 2 at the first two digits. The exact source classification used in the survey materials could not be identified from the available documentation. In the GLD harmonization, the survey coding is therefore mapped conservatively to ISIC Rev. 2 at the 2-digit level.

| Year(s) | Classification in survey | ISIC revision mapped to | Level of compatibility |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| 2013 | Unknown | ISIC Revision 2 | 2 digits |
| 2016 | Unknown | ISIC Revision 2 | 2 digits |

Detailed industry comparisons should be interpreted with this grouped coding structure in mind.
