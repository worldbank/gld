## What is the IND PLFS?

The Indian Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) is the flagship labour force survey conducted by the National Statistical Office (NSO) under the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MOSPI).

Introduced in 2017 as the successor to the Employment and Unemployment Survey (EUS), the PLFS provides India's official statistics on labour force participation, employment and unemployment. Beginning with the 2025 round, the annual PLFS follows the calendar year (January--December), replacing the previous July--June survey cycle.

## What does the PLFS cover?

The PLFS collects information on household characteristics, demographic characteristics, education, labour force participation, employment, unemployment, occupation, industry and status in employment using both the Usual Status (365-day recall) and Current Weekly Status (7-day
recall) concepts.

## Where can the data be found?

The [2025 PLFS microdata are publicly available from the National Data Archive](https://microdata.gov.in/NADA/index.php/catalog/284) (NADA) maintained by MOSPI.

## What is the sampling procedure?

Beginning in January 2025, the PLFS uses a redesigned rotational panel sample. Selected households are interviewed four consecutive months: one first visit followed by three monthly revisits. The redesign supports monthly, quarterly and annual labour market estimates. The redesign also
introduced changes to sampling, stratification and estimation procedures; readers should [consult the official methodology report for details](utilities/IND_2025_LFS_Methodology-Changes.pdf).

## What is the geographic significance level?

-   Monthly estimates: national (with rural/urban breakdowns).
-   Quarterly estimates: India and larger states.
-   Annual estimates: rural/urban level for all states and union
    territories.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### New PLFS series beginning in 2025

The 2025 PLFS begins a new survey series. Besides changing the annual reference period from July--June to January--December, the survey design was comprehensively revised. Users should therefore exercise caution when comparing results with earlier PLFS rounds.

#### Calendar-year reference period

Annual files now correspond to the calendar year rather than July--June.

#### Comparability with previous PLFS rounds

Because both the reference period and the survey methodology changed in 2025, users should exercise caution when comparing estimates with earlier PLFS rounds. [A detailed discussion is provided here](Comparability_of_2025_PLFS_with_Previous_Rounds.md).

#### Monthly rotational panel

Both rural and urban households now participate in a four-month panel consisting of one first visit followed by three monthly revisits.

#### Annual, quarterly and monthly products

Three public-use datasets are released: 

- Annual first-visit microdata (used in the GLD). 
- Quarterly revisit microdata. 
- Monthly revisit microdata (available from April onwards).
