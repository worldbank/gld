# Comparability of the 2025 PLFS with Previous Rounds

## Overview

The 2025 PLFS marks a structural break in the survey series. Comparisons
with earlier annual PLFS rounds should be interpreted carefully because
multiple aspects of the survey changed simultaneously.

## Sources of non-comparability

### Change in reference period

Previous annual PLFS rounds covered July of year *t* through June of
year *t+1*. Beginning in 2025, the annual PLFS covers January through
December.

### Redesigned sample

The 2025 redesign introduces a new sampling strategy, revised
stratification, revised estimation procedures and a substantially larger
sample.

### Rotational panel

The survey now follows households for four consecutive monthly visits.
Earlier designs revisited only urban households quarterly and did not
implement the same national monthly rotation.

### High-frequency estimation

The redesigned survey supports monthly and quarterly estimates using the
new panel design, alongside annual estimates.

## Implications for analysis

Users should avoid interpreting differences between 2025 and earlier
years solely as labour market changes. Observed differences may
reflect: - genuine economic changes, - changes in the survey reference
period, - changes in the sampling design, - changes in estimation
procedures, - or a combination of these factors.

Trend analyses spanning the 2025 redesign should therefore be
interpreted with caution and, where appropriate, supported by robustness
checks.

## Recommended practice

-   Treat 2025 as the beginning of a new analytical series.
-   Clearly document when analyses span the redesign.
