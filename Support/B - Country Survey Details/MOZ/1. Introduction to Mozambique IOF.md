## What is the MOZ IOF?
The Mozambique Household Budget Survey, officially named Inquérito sobre Orçamento Familiar (IOF), is a household-based survey conducted by the National Institute of Statistics of Mozambique. The IOF collects information on household consumption expenditure, income, housing conditions, durable goods, employment, education, health, domestic tourism, financial inclusion, poverty perceptions, and food security. The survey is designed to measure living conditions and support the formulation and monitoring of public policies and development programmes in Mozambique.

## What does the MOZ IOF cover?
The survey collects information on household expenditure and income, including daily, monthly, quarterly, and annual reference periods for different types of consumption. It also includes information on household composition, demographic characteristics, employment, housing and access to basic services, education, health, domestic tourism, financial inclusion, perceptions of poverty, and food security.

The employment module covers household members aged 5 years and older. The survey provides estimates at the national level, national urban and rural levels, provincial level, and by sex.

## Where can the data be found?
The MOZ IOF 2022 datasets are not publicly accessible through an open data portal. The data were shared directly by colleagues in Mozambique for official use and can be shared freely within the authorized institutional context.

However, any reports, tables, or analyses produced using these data should be shared back with the Mozambique colleagues. This allows them to inform the National Statistical Office, strengthen the collaboration, and formally acknowledge the NSO’s support.

For World Bank Group users, access requests can be directed to the Jobs Group at gld@worldbank.org

## What is the sampling procedure?
The 2022 Mozambique Household Budget Survey (IOF 2022) used a probabilistic, stratified, multi-stage sampling design. The sample was drawn from the 2017–2026 master sample, based on the 2017 Population and Housing Census. The survey covers the resident population in Mozambique, excluding people living in collective dwellings.

The sample was designed for national, urban/rural, and provincial estimates. It was selected in three stages: first, 1,496 primary sampling units were selected from the master sample; second, one enumeration area was selected within each selected unit; and third, households were systematically selected within each enumeration area. In urban areas, 12 households were selected per enumeration area, while in rural areas, 9 households were selected.

In total, the planned sample included 1,496 enumeration areas and 15,732 households. A household listing was conducted shortly before fieldwork to update the sampling frame before selecting the final households.

## What is the significance level?
The IOF 2022 was designed to produce estimates at the national, urban/rural, and provincial levels. The provincial domains are Niassa, Cabo Delgado, Nampula, Zambézia, Tete, Manica, Sofala, Inhambane, Gaza, Maputo, and Maputo City.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Employment definition note

The employment definition used in the MOZ IOF 2022 is broadly aligned with the ILO 13th ICLS definition, since it classifies as employed persons aged 15 or older who worked at least one hour during the reference period, helped in the production of goods and services without remuneration, or had a job but were temporarily absent.

However, the IOF 2022 applies some exceptions through an alternative employment definition. In particular, it excludes some cases considered “Type C unemployed”, such as occasional workers, own-account workers without employees and without regular work, and unpaid family workers without regular work. For more details on these exceptions, users should refer to the official [IOF 2022 report, section 6.2](utilities/Manual do Inquiridor IOF 2022 Final_Dezembro 2020_V1.pdf).

This note is included so users take these methodological differences into account when comparing MOZ IOF 2022 labour aggregates with those from other countries. Based on the available survey information, it is not possible to fully reconstruct the original 13th ICLS employment definition from the microdata.

### Unemployment definition note

The IOF 2022 official report uses a broad unemployment definition. According to the report, the ILO unemployment definition includes persons aged 15 or older who, during the survey reference week, were without work, available for work, and seeking employment. However, for IOF 2022, the job-search criterion was waived, meaning that persons without work and available to work may be classified as unemployed even if they did not actively search for a job.

For international comparability, the GLD harmonization uses the strict unemployment definition, which requires that a person is not employed, is available to work, and has actively searched for work. Users who want to reproduce the broader IOF 2022 unemployment definition can construct it using the availability and job-search questions in the employment module:

```
* GLD UNEMPLOYMENT DEFINITION: available AND looking for a job

  gen passive = EM03 == 1
	gen active = inrange(EM07,1,96)
	
	replace lstatus = 2 if passive == 1 & active == 1 & missing(lstatus)
	
	
* IOF 2022 UNEMPLOYMENT DEFINITION: available for a job
* Use the code below instead if you want to apply the broader IOF 2022 definition

  gen passive = EM03 == 1
	
	replace lstatus = 2 if passive == 1 & missing(lstatus)

```


### Education system

This section describes how education levels are recorded in the 2022 Mozambique Household Budget Survey (IOF 2022) and how they are harmonized in the Global Labor Database (GLD).

The survey records the highest level of education completed through `education_level_attended`, originally `AF14A_nivel`, and the highest grade or year completed within that level through `grade_or_year`, originally `AF14_classe`. The education levels include no completed level, pre-school, literacy education, primary education, secondary education, technical education, teacher training, and higher education. 

This information is used to construct `educy`, which measures years of education, and `educat7`, which classifies educational attainment into seven internationally comparable categories. For general education, completed grades 1 to 12 are mapped directly to years of education. Technical and higher education levels are assigned years using harmonization assumptions based on the completed year within the programme.
