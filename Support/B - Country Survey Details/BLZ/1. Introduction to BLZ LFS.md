## What is the BLZ LFS?

The Belize Labour Force Survey (LFS) is the main household survey used to collect information on labour market activities and employment conditions in Belize. The survey was conducted by the national statistical authority, the Central Statistical Office (CSO), which later became the Statistical Institute of Belize (SIB).

The GLD harmonization currently includes the early Belize LFS rounds for 1993, 1994, 1996, 1997, 1998, and 1999. These rounds provide labour market microdata for Belize during the 1990s.

## What does the BLZ LFS cover?

The Belize LFS collects information on household and individual demographic characteristics, education, labour force status, employment, unemployment, occupation, industry, working hours, labour income, and other employment-related characteristics.

The years and sample sizes harmonized for GLD are:

| Year | # of Households | # of Individuals |
| :--- | :-------------- | :--------------- |
| 1993 | 2,991 | 14,122 |
| 1994 | 2,882 | 13,660 |
| 1996 | 3,001 | 13,687 |
| 1997 | 3,175 | 14,781 |
| 1998 | 3,253 | 14,780 |
| 1999 | 3,277 | 15,000 |

## Where can the data be found?

The Belize LFS microdata and documentation are produced by the national statistical authority. For the early LFS rounds, the responsible agency was the Central Statistical Office (CSO). The current national statistical authority is the Statistical Institute of Belize (SIB).

The harmonized GLD files should be accessed through the relevant World Bank / GLD data storage systems. Users should refer to the documentation and harmonization code stored in the Belize country folder for the specific survey year being used.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The Belize LFS is a household sample survey covering both urban and rural areas. Users should consult the original documentation for each early survey round when using sampling weights, strata, PSU variables, or subnational estimates, as the availability and naming of survey design variables may vary across years.

## What is the geographic significance level?

The Belize LFS is intended to produce labour market indicators at the national level. Depending on the survey year and documentation, estimates may also be available by urban/rural area and district. Users should verify the exact geographic representativeness for each early survey round before producing subnational estimates.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Early survey rounds included in GLD

The GLD harmonization currently covers the following early Belize LFS rounds: 1993, 1994, 1996, 1997, 1998, and 1999.

The structure of the raw files and the availability of identifiers, weights, and classification variables may vary across these early rounds. Users should review the harmonization code and year-specific documentation before comparing detailed variables across years.

### Industry classification

The raw industry variable used in the early Belize LFS rounds, for example `p29a` in 1993, appears to follow a Belize national or adapted classification rather than clean standard ISIC Rev. 3 four-digit codes. The documentation and variable references include the Belize Classification of Economic Activities (BCEA).

During harmonization, the industry classification is treated as broadly aligned with ISIC Rev. 3 where possible. At the two-digit level, the classification appears to align reasonably with ISIC categories. However, some detailed codes do not fully pass standard ISIC Rev. 3 validation, suggesting that the raw industry codes may be national/adapted categories, aggregate codes, or codes requiring a separate concordance.

Until a full correspondence table is confirmed, users should be cautious when using detailed four-digit industry categories. More aggregated industry categories are likely to be more reliable than detailed unit-level categories.

### Occupation classification

The raw occupation variable used in the early Belize LFS rounds, for example p28a in 1993, appears broadly related to ISCO-88 but does not fully pass standard ISCO-88 validation at the detailed-code level. Unlike industry, no separate Belize national occupation classification or confirmed correspondence table has yet been identified in the harmonization folder, and the occupation classification version could not be verified from the available documentation. For this reason, the occupation classification version is left uncoded. Users should be cautious when using detailed occupation codes until the classification has been fully confirmed.

### Ongoing classification review

The detailed industry and occupation classifications for the early Belize LFS rounds are still under review. The GLD team has flagged the issue that the raw industry and occupation variables appear to align at broader levels but do not fully pass validation checks at the detailed ISIC/ISCO level.

A country Poverty Economist has been contacted to help confirm the appropriate classification treatment. Until this is resolved, the harmonized files should document the current treatment in the code and use broader industry and occupation categories where possible.

### Documentation

Relevant survey questionnaires, codebooks, classification notes, correspondence files, and any future BCEA/ISCO/ISIC concordance documents should be stored in the Belize documentation or utilities folder. Internal links should use relative paths rather than external web links.
