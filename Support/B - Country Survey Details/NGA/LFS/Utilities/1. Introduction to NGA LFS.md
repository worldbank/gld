## What is the NGA LFS?

The Nigeria Labour Force Survey (LFS), also referred to in the raw documentation as the Nigeria Labour Force Survey (NLFS), is the main household survey used to collect labour market information in Nigeria. The survey is produced by the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS).

The NLFS is designed to collect information on employment, unemployment, labour force participation, labour underutilisation, working time, job characteristics, and other labour-market-related outcomes. The survey provides quarterly labour market statistics and is intended to support policy analysis, government planning, private-sector decision-making, and monitoring of national and international labour market indicators.

## What does the NGA LFS cover?

The Nigeria LFS collects household and individual information relevant to labour market analysis. The questionnaire includes modules on identification, demographic characteristics, disability, education, employment at work, temporary absence, agricultural work and market orientation, main and secondary job characteristics, working time, income, unemployment, and out-of-labour-force status.

The GLD-harmonized files include core variables on:

* household and individual identifiers;
* survey year, quarter, and interview timing;
* survey weights and design variables where available;
* geography, including zone, state, and urban/rural sector;
* age, sex, relationship to household head, marital status, and household size;
* education;
* labour force status;
* unemployment and out-of-labour-force reasons;
* main and secondary job characteristics;
* employment status, occupation, industry, sector, contract, social security, and health insurance variables where available;
* working hours;
* derived broad industry and occupation categories.

## Where can the data be found?

The Nigeria LFS microdata and documentation are available through the National Bureau of Statistics microdata catalog. The relevant catalog entry is titled “Nigeria Labour Force Survey (Q2 2024 - Q2 2025).”

Although the catalog reference ID still refers to Q2 2024, the released data include additional quarters: Q3 and Q4 2024, as well as Q1 and Q2 2025. The GLD harmonization therefore treats the 2024 data as an updated master version and creates a separate partial-year file for 2025.

The harmonized GLD files should be accessed through the relevant World Bank / GLD data storage systems. Users should refer to the documentation, raw data, and harmonization code stored in the Nigeria country folder for the specific survey year and version being used.

## What is the sampling procedure?

The Nigeria LFS is a household sample survey. The public survey documentation describes the annual target sample as 35,520 households, divided across 12 months, with a target of 8,880 households per quarter. The survey covers households across Nigeria’s 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory.

The survey is implemented using face-to-face interviews with Computer-Assisted Personal Interviewing (CAPI). The questionnaire is administered at the household and individual levels, with some modules applying only to eligible household members depending on age or labour market status.

Users should consult the original documentation for each release when using weights, sampling design variables, or subnational estimates. In the harmonized files, `weight_q` corresponds to the quarterly survey weight available in the raw data. For the appended 2024 file, an annualized relative weight is constructed from the quarterly weights to account for the quarter composition of the harmonized file.

## What is the geographic significance level?

The Nigeria LFS covers the national territory and includes geographic identifiers for zone, state, and urban/rural sector. The public metadata lists the geographic coverage as national, zone, state, and sector.

Users should be cautious when producing subnational estimates and should verify the level of representativeness in the original documentation for the relevant quarter and release. In the harmonized files, `subnatid1` corresponds to Nigeria’s geopolitical zones, `subnatid2` corresponds to states including the FCT, and `urban` identifies urban/rural sector.

## Other noteworthy aspects

### Raw file availability

For 2024, the newly released Q3 and Q4 data include individual-level files but do not include separate household identification files. As a result, the harmonization relies on the household and individual identifiers available in the individual files for those quarters.

Household size is derived from the individual roster in the harmonized file rather than taken from a household-level raw file, so that the treatment is consistent across quarters.

### Household and person identifiers

In the appended 2024 file, `hhid` and `pid` alone do not uniquely identify individuals across quarters. There are 18 duplicated `hhid`-`pid` combinations. After reviewing the duplicated cases, they appear to refer to distinct individuals rather than true duplicate observations. The records are therefore kept.

The combination of `hhid`, `pid`, and `qtr` uniquely identifies individuals in the appended 2024 file. Users should keep this in mind when using the harmonized file for checks or analysis involving unique person identifiers across quarters.

### Industry classification

The raw industry variables in the Nigeria LFS are treated as ISIC Rev. 4 in the harmonization. Detailed industry codes are converted to four-digit string codes where available and checked against the ISIC universe.

Some raw codes required minor corrections during harmonization where detailed codes did not pass the ISIC universe check but could be mapped to a valid nearby ISIC Rev. 4 code. Users should rely on the harmonization code for the exact corrections applied. Broader industry categories such as `industrycat10` and `industrycat4` are derived from the harmonized ISIC codes.

### Occupation classification

The raw occupation variables in the Nigeria LFS are treated as ISCO-08 in the harmonization. Detailed occupation codes are converted to four-digit string codes where available and checked against the ISCO universe.

A small number of armed-forces-related occupation codes required correction to valid ISCO-08 codes during harmonization. Users should rely on the harmonization code for the exact corrections applied. The broad occupation variable `occup` is derived from the first digit of the harmonized ISCO-08 code, with armed forces coded separately.

### Documentation

Relevant questionnaires, codebooks, raw data notes, qcheck outputs, classification checks, and harmonization code should be stored in the Nigeria documentation or utilities folder. Internal links should use relative paths rather than external web links.

Users should review the year-specific harmonization code and qcheck outputs before producing detailed estimates, especially for identifiers, agricultural employment coding, industry, occupation, and partial-year comparisons.
